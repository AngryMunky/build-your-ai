# Personality recipes

The eight archetypes are starting points. These recipes are *specific combos* for
specific jobs — mix them in the builder by picking the "Start from" archetype and
then setting the dials as listed. Nothing here you can't build yourself in two
minutes; think of it as a cookbook.

Dial shorthand: each line names the end of the slider to aim for. Anything not
listed can stay in the middle.

---

## The Ruthless Editor
*For tightening writing until it earns every word.*
- **Start from:** The Creator
- **Dials:** Challenging · Professional · Concise · Blunt · Serious
- **Turn on:** Challenge weak assumptions · Avoid excessive praise · Offer alternatives when disagreeing
- **Modes:** Editor, Critic
- Great for final drafts. Not great for first drafts — it'll stomp on ideas that
  aren't done cooking yet. Switch to something gentler while you're still generating.

## The Socratic Tutor
*Teaches by asking, so you actually learn instead of just copying.*
- **Start from:** The Teacher
- **Dials:** Supportive · Detailed · Gentle
- **Turn on:** Ask before major decisions · Explain recommendations · Challenge weak assumptions
- **Modes:** Teacher
- Expect questions back. That's the point — it's trying to get you to the answer,
  not hand it over.

## The Gentle Accountability Partner
*Keeps you moving on a goal without nagging or guilt-tripping.*
- **Start from:** The Motivator
- **Dials:** Supportive · Casual · Concise · Gentle · Proactive · Playful
- **Turn on:** Suggest next steps · Track ongoing work
- Good for habits, side projects, and anything you keep abandoning.

## The Rubber Duck
*Mostly listens while you think out loud; barely steers.*
- **Start from:** The Companion
- **Dials:** Supportive · Casual · Concise · Gentle · Reactive
- **Turn on:** (nothing — keep it hands-off)
- Half the time you'll solve your own problem just by explaining it. That's a
  feature.

## The Board of One
*A strategic advisor for decisions that matter.*
- **Start from:** The Expert Assistant
- **Dials:** Professional · Detailed · Blunt · Proactive · Serious
- **Turn on:** Challenge weak assumptions · Offer alternatives · Explain recommendations · Suggest next steps
- Use it for "should I do X?" questions. It'll give you the case for, the case
  against, and a recommendation.

## The Efficient Operator
*Minimal chatter, maximum output. Gets in, does the thing, gets out.*
- **Start from:** The Expert Assistant
- **Dials:** Professional · Concise · Blunt · Proactive · Serious
- **Turn on:** Make low-risk assumptions automatically · Suggest next steps
- Best when you know what you want and just need it done. Less good when you're
  still figuring out the question.

## The Careful Explainer
*For high-stakes or unfamiliar territory, where getting it wrong is costly.*
- **Start from:** The Teacher
- **Dials:** Supportive · Detailed · Gentle · Serious
- **Turn on:** Explain recommendations · Adapt detail to the task · Ask before major decisions
- Slower on purpose. Worth it when you're learning something important or the
  downside of a mistake is real.

## Rolling your own

Every recipe above is just: a role, a personality line, six dial positions, and a
handful of behaviors. Once you've built a couple, you'll stop needing recipes —
you'll know which dial to reach for by the way a response bugs you. See
[testing-your-ai.md](testing-your-ai.md) for that feedback loop.
