# Testing & refining your AI

A profile isn't finished when you export it — it's finished after you've used it a
bit and fixed what feels off. This is the short version of how to do that without
turning into a full-time prompt engineer.

## Why test at all?

You can't fully predict how a set of instructions will *feel* until you see it
respond. The gap between "sounds right on paper" and "actually pleasant to work
with" is where testing earns its keep. Two or three quick rounds usually gets you
90% of the way there.

## The loop

1. **Paste** your profile into your assistant.
2. **Run a few real prompts** — not test-y ones, the kind of thing you'd actually ask.
3. **Name the defect** in plain words (see the table below).
4. **Adjust one or two dials** in the builder, re-export, paste again.
5. **Repeat** until it stops annoying you.

Change *one thing at a time* where you can. If you flip five settings at once and
it gets worse, you won't know which one did it.

## Test prompts worth trying

Use a spread so you see the personality across situations, not just one:

- **An open idea:** "I'm thinking about starting a ___. Talk it through with me."
- **A routine task:** "Summarize this and pull out the action items." (paste something)
- **A judgment call:** "Here are two options. Which would you pick and why?"
- **A mistake:** ask something slightly wrong on purpose and see if it corrects you.
- **A vague ask:** "Help me with this project." — does it ask good questions or guess?

## Defect → fix

| It feels… | Do this |
|---|---|
| Too wordy | Slide toward **Concise**, or nudge **Shorter** on the Preview step |
| Too agreeable / soft | Slide toward **Challenging**; turn on **Avoid excessive praise** |
| Too cold or robotic | Slide toward **Casual** and/or **Playful**; nudge **Less robotic** |
| Too blunt / harsh | Slide toward **Gentle** |
| Asks too many questions | Turn on **Make low-risk assumptions automatically** |
| Not enough pushback | Turn on **Challenge weak assumptions** |
| Doesn't take initiative | Slide toward **Proactive**; turn on **Suggest next steps** |
| Makes wrong assumptions | Turn on **Ask before major decisions** |
| Explains too little | Turn on **Explain recommendations**; slide toward **Detailed** |

## Watch for over-correction

The most common mistake is fixing one thing so hard you create the opposite
problem. Dial it back a notch, not all the way. "A bit too agreeable" doesn't need
"ruthless critic" — it needs one step toward challenging.

## Revising after real use

Come back after a week. The things that actually bug you in daily use are more
honest signal than anything you'll notice in a test session. Adjust, re-export,
move on. Your profile is a living document, not a monument.
